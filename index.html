Project Overview

This project analyzes emergency response patterns in New York City using public data from the Fire Department of New York City (FDNY). The visualizations explore EMS and Fire Dispatch data, highlighting spatial and temporal trends.

The goal is to communicate key insights visually and clearly — without requiring technical knowledge — so that anyone, even non-data people, can engage with the findings.

Genre: Hybrid data story — combining narrative structure with reader-driven exploration.

Target Audience: Curious non-technical users, fellow DTU students, or citizens interested in NYC emergency data.
